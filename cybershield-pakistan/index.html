What is happening to you?

Cybercrime takes many forms. Learn about each one and what you can do if it is happening to you.

💬

Cyberbullying

Being harassed, threatened, humiliated, or repeatedly targeted online by individuals or groups.
📷

Picture and video leaking

Your private or intimate images being shared online without your knowledge or consent.
🔓

Account hacking

Someone gaining unauthorized access to your social media, email, or other online accounts.
👤

Impersonation and fake accounts

Someone creating a fake account pretending to be you to deceive others or damage your reputation.
⚠

Blackmail and sextortion

Being threatened or pressured to pay money or perform acts under threat of exposing private content.
👁

Online stalking

Someone persistently following, monitoring, or contacting you online in a way that causes fear or distress.

Your rights under Pakistani law

Under PECA 2016, cybercrime is a serious criminal offence in Pakistan. You have powerful rights as a victim.

5 years

Maximum jail sentence for sharing intimate images without consent under PECA 2016 Section 21A.

Rs. 5M

Maximum fine for non-consensual image sharing. Harassers face serious financial and criminal consequences.

24 hours

Time within which platforms must remove harmful content after a valid complaint to PTA.

We are building something important.

CyberShield Pakistan is currently in its awareness phase. We are educating the public about cybercrime rights under PECA 2016 and building formal partnerships with authorities and support organizations including FIA Cyber Crime Wing, PTA, Rozan Pakistan, and Digital Rights Foundation.

Our full complaint submission and case management system will launch once we have confirmed support pathways for every victim who comes to us. We refuse to accept complaints we cannot properly support. That is our commitment to you.

1

Phase 3 — Complaint system

Full secure complaint submission and tracking system goes live.

What happened to you is not your fault.

We are building this platform with patience and care because the people who will use it deserve nothing less than a system that truly works.
