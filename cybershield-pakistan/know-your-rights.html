PECA 2016 — Pakistan Electronic Crimes Act

Know your rights as a victim of cybercrime in Pakistan

Understanding your rights is the first step toward getting justice. These laws exist to protect you.

What is a crime under Pakistani law?

Under PECA 2016 these actions are criminal offences punishable by jail time and heavy fines.

Section 21A

Non-consensual sharing of intimate images

Sharing, publishing, or distributing intimate or private images of a person without their consent is a serious criminal offence regardless of how the images were originally obtained.

Punishment: Up to 5 years imprisonment and Rs. 5 million fine or both.

Section 20

Online harassment and defamation

Sending, posting, or sharing any information through an information system that is false and intended to harm the reputation or privacy of a person, or causes fear, distress, or harassment.

Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment and Rs. 1 million fine or both.

Section 21

Cyberstalking

Continuously following, monitoring, contacting, or watching a person online in a way that causes them to feel fear, distress, or anxiety. This includes monitoring someone's online activity without their knowledge.

Punishment: Up to 1 year imprisonment or Rs. 1 million fine or both.

Section 16

Unauthorized access — hacking

Accessing, obtaining, or interfering with data or an information system without authorization from the owner. This includes hacking into social media accounts, email, or any other digital platform.

Punishment: Up to 3 months imprisonment or Rs. 50,000 fine or both.

Section 22

Impersonation and fake accounts

Creating a fake identity, fake account, or fake profile to deceive others, damage someone's reputation, or gain access to information. Using someone else's name, photos, or identity online without permission.

Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment or fine or both.

Your rights as a victim

1

You have the right to report anonymously

You do not have to reveal your identity when reporting a cybercrime. Your name and personal details can be kept completely private throughout the reporting process.

2

You have the right to have harmful content removed within 24 hours

Under PTA regulations, social media platforms and websites are required to remove harmful, illegal, or non-consensual content within 24 hours of receiving a valid complaint. You can file directly at complaints.pta.gov.pk.

3

You have the right to a protection order

If you feel your physical safety is at risk as a result of online harassment or threats, you can apply for a protection order through FIA Cyber Crime Wing. This legally restricts the harasser from contacting you.

4

You have the right to free legal consultation

Digital Rights Foundation provides completely free legal advice to victims of cyber harassment through their helpline 0800-39393. You do not need money to access legal guidance.

5

You have the right to be treated with dignity

Every authority and support organization is required to treat you with respect and without judgment throughout the reporting and legal process. Your experience and safety matter.

What to do right now — step by step

If you are experiencing any form of online abuse, follow these steps immediately.

1

Do not delete anything

Screenshot every message, comment, post, profile, username, and URL related to the abuse. This evidence is crucial for any official report or legal action. Evidence that is deleted cannot be recovered.

2

Screenshot first then block

Before you block the person on any platform, take screenshots of their profile, their username, their posts, and any messages they sent you. Once you block them you may lose access to this information.

3

Write down everything you remember

Write the dates and times of every incident you can remember. Include what was said or done, which platforms it happened on, and any witnesses who saw the abuse. Keep this written record somewhere safe.

4

Tell one trusted person

Tell a family member, close friend, teacher, or someone you trust completely what is happening. You should not carry this alone. Having someone who knows also means having a witness to your situation.

5

Report to the appropriate authority

Contact FIA Cyber Crime Wing on 0800-02345 for law enforcement action. Contact Digital Rights Foundation on 0800-39393 for free legal advice. Contact PTA at complaints.pta.gov.pk for content removal. CyberShield's complaint system is coming soon.

6

Look after your mental health

Online abuse causes real emotional harm. Contact Rozan Pakistan at info@rozan.org for counseling and emotional support. You do not have to be strong all the time. Getting support is a sign of strength not weakness.

What NOT to do — very important

⚠

Do not pay blackmailers

Paying someone who is blackmailing you never stops the abuse. It tells them their threat worked and they will demand more. No matter how frightening the threat, do not pay. Report immediately.

🗑

Do not delete evidence

Even if you want the messages gone, do not delete them before reporting. Evidence is the foundation of any legal case. Screenshot everything first. You can delete after reporting if you need to.

📢

Do not share publicly before reporting

Do not post about your case on social media before it has been investigated. This can compromise your case, alert the harasser, and make legal action more difficult.

💬

Do not confront the harasser online

Responding to or confronting the person harassing you online usually makes the situation worse. It gives them more material and more power. Collect evidence and report through official channels.
