What is cyberbullying and what are your rights in Pakistan?

Cyberbullying is when someone uses digital technology — social media, messaging apps, online games, or any other online platform — to repeatedly harass, threaten, embarrass, or target another person. It is not a single incident. It is a pattern of behavior intended to cause harm, fear, or distress.

What does cyberbullying look like?

  • Sending threatening or abusive messages repeatedly through WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, or any other platform.
  • Posting embarrassing photos, videos, or information about someone without their permission.
  • Creating fake accounts to impersonate someone or spread false information about them.
  • Excluding someone from online groups deliberately to cause isolation and distress.
  • Encouraging others to harass a specific person — sometimes called pile-on harassment.
  • Sharing someone's private information publicly — sometimes called doxing.
  • Commenting repeatedly on someone's posts with abusive, humiliating, or threatening language.

How it affects victims

Cyberbullying causes serious psychological harm. Victims commonly experience anxiety, depression, loss of self-confidence, difficulty sleeping, withdrawal from social life, and in severe cases thoughts of self-harm. The harm is real even though it happens online. Anyone who tells you to simply ignore it or put your phone down does not understand the seriousness of what you are experiencing.

What is illegal under PECA 2016

Under Section 20 of PECA 2016, sending information through an information system that is intended to harm someone's reputation or cause them fear, distress, or harassment is a criminal offence. The punishment is up to 3 years imprisonment and Rs. 1 million fine or both. This law applies to messages sent on WhatsApp, posts on Facebook and Instagram, comments on TikTok, and any other digital platform.

What to do if you are being cyberbullied

  1. Do not delete anything — Screenshot every message, comment, post, profile, username, and URL related to the abuse.
  2. Screenshot first then block — Before you block the person, capture their profile and messages.
  3. Write down everything you remember — Dates, times, platforms, and witnesses.
  4. Tell one trusted person — You should not carry this alone.
  5. Report to the appropriate authority — FIA 0800-02345, DRF 0800-39393, PTA complaints.pta.gov.pk.
  6. Look after your mental health — Contact Rozan at info@rozan.org for support.
