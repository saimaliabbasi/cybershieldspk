How to collect and preserve evidence before reporting a cybercrime

Evidence is the foundation of any successful cybercrime report or legal case. Without evidence, authorities have very little to work with. The good news is that collecting digital evidence is something you can do yourself right now with your phone or computer. Here is exactly what to collect and how.

What counts as evidence

  • Screenshots of abusive, threatening, or harassing messages.
  • Screenshots of fake accounts or impersonation profiles.
  • URLs and links to harmful posts or content.
  • Usernames, profile names, and profile pictures of the person harassing you.
  • Phone numbers and email addresses used to contact you.
  • Dates and times of every incident.
  • Names of any witnesses who saw the abuse.
  • Any payment receipts if you were scammed or extorted.

How to take proper screenshots

On your phone press the power button and volume down button at the same time. On iPhone press the side button and volume up button together. Make sure your screenshots show the full message including the sender's name or username, the date and time stamp, and the full content. Do not crop screenshots before reporting.

How to save and organize your evidence

Create a folder on your phone or computer called Evidence. Save all screenshots into this folder. Write a simple document listing every incident with dates, times, platforms, and what happened. Send copies of everything to a trusted email address so you have a backup even if your phone is lost or damaged.

What NOT to do with evidence

  • Do not edit, filter, or modify any screenshots.
  • Do not delete original messages after screenshotting.
  • Do not share evidence publicly on social media before reporting.
  • Do not send evidence to the harasser as a warning.

Once you have collected your evidence you are ready to report. Contact FIA on 0800-02345 or Digital Rights Foundation on 0800-39393 for guidance on next steps.
