PECA 2016 — Pakistan's cybercrime law explained in simple language

The Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act 2016 — commonly called PECA 2016 — is Pakistan's main law against cybercrime. It was passed by the National Assembly in 2016 and gives authorities the power to investigate, prosecute, and punish a wide range of online crimes. Most Pakistanis do not know what this law says. That means most victims do not know they have legal protection. This article explains the most important sections in plain simple language.

Section 21A — Non-consensual sharing of intimate images

Sharing, publishing, or distributing intimate or private images without consent is a serious criminal offence. Punishment: Up to 5 years imprisonment and Rs. 5 million fine or both.

Section 20 — Online harassment and defamation

Sending information that harms reputation or causes fear, distress, or harassment. Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment and Rs. 1 million fine or both.

Section 21 — Cyberstalking

Continuously following, monitoring, or contacting someone online causing fear or distress. Punishment: Up to 1 year imprisonment or Rs. 1 million fine or both.

Section 16 — Unauthorized access (hacking)

Accessing data or systems without authorization. Punishment: Up to 3 months imprisonment or Rs. 50,000 fine or both.

Section 22 — Impersonation and fake accounts

Creating fake identities or profiles to deceive others or damage reputation. Punishment: Up to 3 years imprisonment or fine or both.

How to use this law

To use PECA 2016 you do not need a lawyer to start. You can contact FIA Cyber Crime Wing directly on their helpline 0800-02345 or visit your nearest FIA Cyber Crime Circle office. Bring screenshots and a written account of what happened. For free legal guidance before approaching FIA contact Digital Rights Foundation on 0800-39393.
