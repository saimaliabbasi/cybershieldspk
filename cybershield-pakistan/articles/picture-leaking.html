My pictures or videos were leaked — what do I do right now?

If your private or intimate images have been shared without your consent, the first thing we want you to know is this — what happened to you is a crime in Pakistan. You are the victim. You have done nothing wrong. And there are real, practical steps you can take right now.

Is this a crime in Pakistan?

Yes. Under Section 21A of PECA 2016, sharing intimate or private images of a person without their consent is a serious criminal offence. It does not matter how the images were originally taken or shared with the person. It does not matter if you trusted them at the time. Sharing them without your current consent is illegal. The punishment is up to 5 years imprisonment and Rs. 5 million fine. This is one of the most serious cybercrime offences under Pakistani law.

Do these things immediately — right now

  1. Do not panic. We know this is terrifying. But a clear head will help you take the right steps.
  2. Document everything immediately. Screenshot the posts, profiles, links, usernames, and any messages related to the sharing of your images.
  3. Report to the platform. Every major platform has a reporting system for non-consensual intimate images.
  4. Report to PTA. Go to complaints.pta.gov.pk and file a complaint.
  5. Contact Digital Rights Foundation. Call 0800-39393 for free legal advice.
  6. Contact FIA Cyber Crime Wing. Call 0800-02345 to file an official criminal complaint.
  7. Do not confront the person who shared your images. Go directly to authorities.

What NOT to do

  • Do not pay anyone who is threatening to share more images.
  • Do not delete messages or evidence before reporting.
  • Do not share the situation on your own social media before the case is investigated.
  • Do not blame yourself. You are the victim of a crime.

Protecting yourself going forward

After reporting, change the passwords to all your accounts. Enable two-factor authentication on every platform. Review who has access to your photos and videos. Consider making your social media accounts private temporarily.

You have rights. You have support. CyberShield is here and so are the organizations we have listed above. You are not alone.
