My account was hacked — step by step recovery guide

Having your social media or email account hacked is one of the most violating experiences online. Someone now has access to your private messages, photos, contacts, and identity. You need to act immediately and in the right order.

Do these things immediately — in this exact order

Step 1 — Try to log back in immediately. If you can still get in, change your password immediately from a different device than the one you normally use. Use a completely new strong password — minimum 12 characters with uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.

Step 2 — If you cannot log in, use the platform's account recovery. For Instagram go to the login page and click "Forgot password". For Facebook click "Find your account". For Gmail go to accounts.google.com/signin/recovery.

Step 3 — Enable two-factor authentication immediately after regaining access.

Step 4 — Check connected apps and sessions. Log out all sessions except your current device. Remove any apps you do not recognize.

Step 5 — Alert your contacts that your account was hacked.

Step 6 — Check what was accessed in messages, posts, and account activity.

Step 7 — Report to FIA on 0800-02345 if sensitive information was accessed or the hacker is harming others using your account.

How to prevent hacking in future

Use a different strong password for every account. Never use the same password twice. Enable two-factor authentication on every account right now. Never click links in messages from people you do not know. Never enter your password on a website unless you are certain it is the real website. Do not share your password with anyone.
