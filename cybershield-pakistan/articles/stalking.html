Online stalking — your rights and what to do in Pakistan

Online stalking is when someone persistently follows, monitors, or contacts you online in a way that causes fear or distress. This can include repeatedly messaging you, watching your activity, creating new accounts after you block them, or using location or personal information to intimidate you.

What is illegal under PECA 2016

Under Section 21, continuously following, monitoring, contacting, or watching a person online in a way that causes fear, distress, or anxiety is cyberstalking — a criminal offence punishable by up to 1 year imprisonment or Rs. 1 million fine or both.

What to do right now

  1. Document every incident with screenshots, dates, and times.
  2. Review your privacy settings on all platforms and restrict who can see your location and posts.
  3. Block the stalker on every platform — after saving evidence.
  4. Report to FIA Cyber Crime Wing: 0800-02345.
  5. Ask about a protection order if you fear for your physical safety.
  6. Contact Rozan Pakistan at info@rozan.org for emotional support.

What NOT to do

Do not confront the stalker online. Do not share your location publicly. Do not delete evidence before reporting.
