Someone is blackmailing me online — do not pay, here is what to do

If someone is threatening to share your private images, personal information, or anything else about you unless you pay money or do something against your will — this is called blackmail or sextortion and it is a serious crime in Pakistan. We know you are scared. We know the threat feels overwhelming. But we need you to read this carefully because the decision you make in the next few hours matters enormously.

The most important thing first — do not pay

We cannot say this strongly enough. Do not pay. Paying a blackmailer never stops the blackmail. The moment you pay, you prove to them that their threat works and that you are willing to pay. They will come back with more demands. Paying is the one thing that guarantees the abuse continues. The path through this is not payment — it is reporting.

What is happening to you is a crime

Under PECA 2016 Section 24, using digital means to threaten, extort, or coerce someone is a criminal offence. Under Section 21A, threatening to share intimate images without consent is punishable by up to 5 years imprisonment and Rs. 5 million fine. You are the victim of a crime. They are the criminal.

What to do right now

  1. Do not respond to the blackmailer. Do not negotiate or threaten them back.
  2. Screenshot everything immediately — every threat, message, username, and profile link.
  3. Do not send money. Not even a small amount.
  4. Report to FIA immediately. Call 0800-02345.
  5. Contact Digital Rights Foundation on 0800-39393.
  6. Tell someone you trust. Do not carry this alone.

Protecting yourself

After reporting, block the blackmailer on all platforms. Make your social media accounts private. Change passwords on all accounts. Enable two-factor authentication. If they contact you through a new account, screenshot and report again.

You have done nothing wrong. This person is a criminal. You have the law on your side and you have support. Please report immediately.
