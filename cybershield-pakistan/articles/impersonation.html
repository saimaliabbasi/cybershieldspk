Impersonation and fake accounts — what to do in Pakistan

When someone creates a fake account using your name, photos, or identity to deceive others or damage your reputation, it is not just upsetting — it is a crime under Pakistani law. You have the right to report this and have the account removed.

What is illegal under PECA 2016

Under Section 22, creating a fake identity, fake account, or fake profile to deceive others, damage someone's reputation, or gain access to information is a criminal offence. Punishment can include up to 3 years imprisonment or a fine or both.

What to do right now

  1. Screenshot the fake profile, username, URL, and any posts or messages.
  2. Report the account to the platform using their impersonation reporting tool.
  3. File a complaint at complaints.pta.gov.pk for faster removal.
  4. Contact FIA Cyber Crime Wing on 0800-02345 with your evidence.
  5. Contact Digital Rights Foundation on 0800-39393 for free legal guidance.
  6. Warn close contacts that a fake account is impersonating you — do not engage with the impersonator.

What NOT to do

Do not message the fake account owner directly. Do not delete evidence before reporting. Do not post publicly about the case before authorities have been contacted if you plan to pursue legal action.

What happened to you is not your fault. You have rights under PECA 2016 and support is available. Read your full rights →
